Last [time](https://fergusfinn.com/blog/profile-guided-adaptive-speculation/) I
priced the cost of verifying a speculated token, and the expert tax was a big
part of it. That model treated each token's choice of experts as an independent
draw: a verify step over $N$ positions activates a coupon-collector's worth of
distinct experts, and at small batch each extra speculated token drags in mostly
fresh experts and amortises almost nothing. {/* TODO: one line linking to #the-expert-tax */}

That independence assumption is the thing worth a second look. Speculated tokens
are not independent draws. They are a contiguous run of one sequence's own text.
